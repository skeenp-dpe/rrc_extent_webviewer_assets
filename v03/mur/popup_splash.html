Murray River (Yarrawonga Weir to the Wakool Junction)

Welcome to the Reconnecting River Country Program (RRCP) inundation interactive map. The map provides access to the program's modelled outputs showing potential inundation for the flow rates being investigated.

This app focuses on the Murray River between the Yarrawonga Weir and its confluence with the Wakool River. The default flow option displayed in the app is 15,000 ML/d at Yarrawonga Weir, which is the current temporary operational limit.

Whats new

The interactive map has been updated to simplify accessing inundation information about your property. The updates include:

  1. Model zones merged to provide seamless mapping for each project area
  2. Modelled levees and embankments have been added to the mapping
  3. Modelled flows at gauges are now provided in table form to allow easier viewing
  4. Gauge board diagrams added for some gauges. Remaining gauges will be added in the future once analysis is complete
  5. Additional text added to the banner to describe potential frequency, timing, and duration of flows
  6. Additional text added to describe assumptions regarding Goulburn River inflows

Using the app

To show different flow options, choose one of the layers and it will show you on the map the changing inundation.

You can also zoom and pan around the map using your mouse and the included tools:

Imagery options can be toggled in the Layer Control and can be displayed underneath inundation extents:

Satellite

Composite satellite imagery represents a collection of recent satellite photographs captured and delivered in high resolution.

Basemap

A cartographic layer from OpenStreetMap representing physical features on the ground such as roads, rail, locailities, national parks and other features across the landscape.

2021 Event

Aerial photography of a recent natural flow event between August and December 2021. This imagery was only captured for some areas of the program by the NSW Department of Planning and Environment.

Bridges

The mapping shows the extent of inundation, including the presence of water under road, highway and rail bridges. For bridges, the mapping does not accurately indicate inundation. The mapping product is overlain over aerial imagery which may appear to show bridges as being overtopped by water, but may actually represent water under the bridges.
