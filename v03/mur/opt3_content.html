Details

This option is for a flow limit of 30,000 ML/d at Yarrawonga Weir. The mapping shows the upper limit of inundation at this flow rate (light blue) plus an inundation extent that would be more typically experienced when environmental flow releases are made (dark blue). Two extents are shown to demonstrate that inundation outcomes associated with investigated flow releases will be variable in response to catchment conditions and will typically be less than the upper limit.

Notes

1The upper limit inundation extent assumes catchment conditions are wet (low infiltration and evaporation losses), and flows are held at this level for an extended duration. Environmental flow releases to this inundation extent under this flow limit option will be infrequent or rare.

2The more typical inundation represented on the maps is the extent of the inundation that may be expected for most environmental flow releases under this flow limit option.

Frequency, timing and duration

The frequency of managed environmental flows enabled by the program (ie higher flows greater than 22,000 ML/d to water wetlands and low-lying floodplain, as mapped here) would vary over time depending on water availability, climate and ecological conditions. On average, over the long-term, the program anticipates these higher environmental flow deliveries would occur about 4 to 5 years per decade, comprising some smaller events and some larger events up to the flow limit. These flows would include some new events and some events that extend the duration of existing unregulated events using water for the environment. These higher environmental flow deliveries would not occur during extreme wet conditions when there is elevated flood risk.

Most of these higher environmental flow deliveries would occur from August to October. Occasionally, and when conditions are appropriate, deliveries might occur slightly earlier in May to July, or extend slightly later into November. Deliveries extending into November would be infrequent, and mostly involve augmenting an existing unregulated flow event to improve environmental outcomes or manage hypoxic blackwater risk.

The duration of these higher environmental flow deliveries would be up to 5 days at the peak flow rate, followed by a gradual reduction in flows back to background flow levels.

Assumptions regarding Billabong Creek flows

Managed environmental flows in the Murrumbidgee River associated with these flow limit options would contribute flows into Yanco Creek and subsequently into Billabong Creek via Colombo Creek. Managed environmental flows that reach Billabong Creek through Colombo Creek would be small relative to flows originating from the Billabong Creek catchment upstream of Colombo Creek. The Billabong Creek catchment upstream of Colombo Creek would remain the primary contributor of high flows in Billabong Creek through the Project area.

There is however potential for these managed environmental flows to coincide with high flows originating from the Billabong Creek catchment. The modelling has allowed for up to 2,300 ML/d contribution from the unregulated Billabong Creek upstream of Colombo Creek (including via the Nowrannie Creek anabranch), which is approximately the 90th percentile flow during the period August to October when managed environmental flow deliveries would occur. This allows for managed environmental flow deliveries under a wide range of conditions whilst noting that managed environmental flow deliveries would not occur during extreme wet conditions when there is elevated flood risk.
